Examples of Useful Resources for Diversity Scholars

On this page you will find resources that may be useful to diversity scholars whether seasoned or just starting their research career. These resources are listed in no specific order. Additionally, you are welcome to complete the form below to leave feedback on the resources provided.

Example Description Link
Consent form language/templates Consent form language/templates for various types of data sharing (raw data, de-identified data, sharing data with other researchers, publicly, etc.) See for example the Qualitative Data Repository’s templates.
Case Studies Case studies of underlying data being shared back to research participants in intentionally useful ways for different disciplines. --
Checklist of Questions A checklist of questions to consider in making decisions throughout the research process, including how far to open data, and to whom. See for example the Data Ethics Canvas.
Hidden Identifying Information Resources regarding potential hidden identifying information or embedded metadata. For example, underlying geotagging in the “Healthy Minds” study was used to identify survey respondent locations and ultimately shut down a dormthat typically housed minority and art students.
Data Management practices Best practices for specific data management practices such as data cleaning, version control/setting up workflows, de-identification of human subjects data, etc. --
Sharing Data Resources specifically for best practices in sharing data (publicly or otherwise) --
Accessibility guidelines Accessibility guidelines or resources for formatting and/or hosting data accessibility for those using assistive technologies, etc. --
Resources for research participants Resources for research participants to understand their rights around their own data and participation. See for example the Chicago Beyond guidebook, “Why Am I Always Being Researched?”.
Reading Material Reading material on ethical considerations for data decisions in various stages of research. See for example the Responsible Data Handbook, or consider something like a bibliography of articles.
Data Applications and Data Use Agreements Templates for one-page data applications and/or data use agreements or guidelines for data you may want to share with others. --
Engaging Communities Examples of successful projects engaging communities in research design and data governance. See for example the “Indigenous Data Sovereignty and Governance in practice” section of “Good Data Practices for Indigenous Data Sovereignty” chapter
